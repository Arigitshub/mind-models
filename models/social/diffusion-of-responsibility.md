# Diffusion of Responsibility

**Category:** social
**Origin:** John Darley & Bibb Latane, 1968
**Tags:** responsibility, bystander, groups, inaction, social

---

## Summary

Diffusion of responsibility is the tendency for individual action to decrease when multiple people are present, because each person assumes someone else will act.

---

## Mechanism

1. A situation appears to require intervention or ownership
2. Multiple observers are aware of the same situation
3. Responsibility feels distributed across the group
4. Each person waits for someone else to move first
5. Inaction by others becomes a signal that action may not be necessary
6. Collective passivity emerges from individually deferred responsibility

---

## Triggers

- Public emergencies with many witnesses
- Group tasks with ambiguous ownership
- Large teams where accountability is diffuse
- Situations where nobody is explicitly assigned to respond

---

## Effects

- Slower intervention in emergencies
- Missed ownership in teams and organizations
- Moral disengagement through shared passivity
- Escalation of small problems because no one claims them early

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Bystander street incident:**
People hesitate to intervene because they assume someone else nearby will call for help.

**Example 2 - Shared inbox:**
Critical requests sit unanswered because each teammate assumes another person will respond.

**Example 3 - Production bug:**
A visible issue persists because ownership is spread across multiple teams and no one takes the first step.

---

## Counters

- Assign explicit ownership in group settings
- In emergencies, address one person directly rather than appealing to the crowd
- Treat visible inaction as a risk signal, not proof that action is unnecessary
