# Groupthink

**Category:** social
**Origin:** Irving Janis, 1972
**Tags:** groups, consensus, conformity, decisions, pressure

---

## Summary

Groupthink is a failure mode where the desire for harmony and consensus suppresses dissent, critical evaluation, and realistic appraisal inside a decision-making group.

---

## Mechanism

1. A cohesive group faces a high-stakes decision
2. Pressure for agreement grows stronger than pressure for accuracy
3. Dissent feels socially costly or disloyal
4. Alternative views are filtered out or self-censored
5. The group mistakes internal agreement for correctness
6. Poor decisions survive because critique never fully enters the room

---

## Triggers

- Strong group cohesion or identity
- Directive leadership that signals a preferred answer
- High stress and urgency
- Isolation from outside critique
- Norms that treat dissent as disloyalty

---

## Effects

- Weak evaluation of alternatives
- Overconfidence in flawed plans
- Illusions of unanimity
- Suppressed warnings and red flags

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Executive meeting:**
A leadership team converges too quickly on an acquisition because no one wants to be the lone skeptic in front of the CEO.

**Example 2 - Product launch:**
A team ignores repeated QA concerns because momentum and optimism have become part of the group's identity.

**Example 3 - Policy failure:**
Officials filter out contrary intelligence because agreement inside the inner circle feels more reassuring than uncertainty.

---

## Counters

- Assign explicit dissent roles in major decisions
- Gather independent judgments before discussion
- Bring in outside reviewers with no stake in group cohesion
