# Conformity

**Category:** social
**Origin:** Solomon Asch, 1951
**Tags:** conformity, pressure, groups, norms, influence

---

## Summary

Conformity is the tendency to align beliefs or behavior with group norms, either to fit in socially or because the group is treated as a source of information.

---

## Mechanism

1. A person observes a group's expressed belief or behavior
2. The group signal creates social and informational pressure
3. The person weighs personal judgment against the cost of deviating
4. Public or private alignment with the group occurs
5. Repeated conformity reinforces the visible norm for everyone else

---

## Triggers

- Clear majority consensus
- Ambiguous situations where the correct answer is uncertain
- Desire for belonging or fear of social friction
- Public responses where deviation is visible

---

## Effects

- Group norms become self-reinforcing
- Individual judgment is suppressed
- Errors can spread collectively when dissent collapses
- Coordination improves when the norm is actually useful

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Meeting silence:**
Team members stay quiet about a risky proposal because everyone else seems supportive.

**Example 2 - Fashion and slang:**
People adopt styles or language to signal membership in a group.

**Example 3 - Classroom answer:**
A student changes a correct answer after hearing the rest of the class confidently choose a different one.

---

## Counters

- Create visible permission for dissent
- Collect independent judgments before group discussion
- Separate public agreement from private belief in analysis
