# Theory of Mind

**Category:** developmental
**Origin:** Premack & Woodruff, 1978; Wimmer & Perner, 1983
**Tags:** development, perspective, beliefs, social-cognition, learning

---

## Summary

Theory of mind is the developmental capacity to understand that other people have beliefs, knowledge, intentions, and perspectives that can differ from one's own.

---

## Mechanism

1. The child begins with limited differentiation between self and other perspectives
2. Social interaction provides repeated evidence that others know and believe different things
3. Perspective-taking capacity becomes more explicit and flexible
4. The child starts reasoning about false beliefs, hidden knowledge, and intention
5. More advanced social prediction and communication become possible

---

## Triggers

- Social play involving deception, guessing, or hidden information
- Conversations about what different people know or think
- Developmental stages where false-belief tasks become tractable
- Caregiver explanation of motives and perspectives

---

## Effects

- Better empathy and social prediction
- Improved communication and teaching
- Greater ability to understand deception and misunderstanding
- More sophisticated interpretation of motives and beliefs

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - False-belief task:**
A child begins to understand that another person will search where they last saw an object, not where the child now knows it is.

**Example 2 - Classroom explanation:**
A student realizes a peer is confused because they lack information, not because they are irrational.

**Example 3 - Team communication:**
An adult communicator anticipates what the audience knows and explains accordingly.

---

## Counters

- Do not assume perspective-taking ability is fully developed just because language is advanced
- Make hidden assumptions explicit in teaching and collaboration
- Use questions that surface what other people currently know, not just what they should know

---

## Related Models

- [Social Learning Theory](social-learning-theory.md) - observing others is more powerful when their internal states can be inferred
- [Attachment Theory](attachment-theory.md) - early social regulation shapes later understanding of minds and intentions
