# Emotional Contagion

**Category:** emotional
**Origin:** Elaine Hatfield, John Cacioppo, & Richard Rapson, 1993
**Tags:** emotion, contagion, empathy, groups, affect

---

## Summary

Emotional contagion is the spread of affect from one person or group to another through automatic mimicry, synchronization, and shared interpretation.

---

## Mechanism

1. A person observes another's emotional expression, tone, posture, or pace
2. Subtle mimicry and attentional alignment occur automatically
3. The mirrored signals feed back into the observer's own affective state
4. Shared interpretation amplifies the spread across a group
5. The emotional climate becomes self-reinforcing

---

## Triggers

- Close social proximity or high attentional focus
- Highly expressive leaders or peers
- Stressful or uncertain situations
- Digital environments with strong emotional cues and repetition

---

## Effects

- Mood spreads across teams, crowds, or audiences
- Anxiety and outrage can escalate quickly
- Calm and confidence can stabilize a group
- Individual judgment becomes colored by collective emotion

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Team standup:**
One visibly stressed manager can make the whole room more tense before any facts are discussed.

**Example 2 - Viral outrage:**
Repeated exposure to angry posts increases a user's own anger even before they engage analytically with the issue.

**Example 3 - Customer service floor:**
One upbeat agent can lift the energy of nearby coworkers and improve overall tone.

---

## Counters

- Slow emotional spread with deliberate pauses and lower-intensity signaling
- Avoid treating a contagious group mood as objective evidence
- Use calm procedural structure during tense group decisions

---

## Related Models

- [Social Proof](../social/social-proof.md) - emotional states spread partly because people look to others for cues
- [Affect Heuristic](../decision/affect-heuristic.md) - shared emotion changes how risks and benefits feel
