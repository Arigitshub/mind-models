# Dopamine Reward Prediction

**Category:** emotional
**Origin:** Wolfram Schultz, 1997
**Tags:** dopamine, reward, prediction, learning, motivation

---

## Summary

Dopamine reward prediction describes how the brain updates motivation and learning based on the gap between expected reward and actual reward.

---

## Mechanism

1. The brain forms an expectation about a future reward
2. An outcome occurs
3. If the reward is better than expected, dopamine response increases
4. If the reward matches expectation, the spike diminishes
5. If the reward is worse than expected or absent, dopamine response drops
6. The system updates what cues and actions are worth repeating

---

## Triggers

- Uncertain rewards with variable timing or magnitude
- Novel stimuli that could signal opportunity
- Cues previously associated with positive outcomes
- Environments with rapid feedback loops

---

## Effects

- Strong learning from surprise
- Increased motivation for variable reward systems
- Cue sensitivity that can drive craving and compulsive behavior
- Shifting attention from reward itself to the predictors of reward

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Slot machine behavior:**
Irregular payouts produce stronger motivation than predictable ones because the reward prediction error stays active.

**Example 2 - Social notifications:**
Intermittent likes or messages keep people checking their phones because each check might produce an unexpected reward.

**Example 3 - Training feedback:**
Immediate positive feedback after a difficult task increases the chance the same strategy is repeated.

---

## Counters

- Reduce exposure to variable reward environments when trying to break compulsive loops
- Make healthy rewards more immediate and visible
- Separate short-term dopamine pull from long-term value in decisions
