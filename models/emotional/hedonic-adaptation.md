# Hedonic Adaptation

**Category:** emotional
**Origin:** Brickman & Campbell, 1971
**Tags:** adaptation, happiness, emotion, baseline, change

---

## Summary

Hedonic adaptation is the tendency for emotional reactions to gains and losses to fade over time as people return toward a relatively stable baseline of well-being.

---

## Mechanism

1. A positive or negative life change occurs
2. Emotion shifts sharply in response to the event
3. Attention and novelty are initially high
4. Over time the new condition becomes normal
5. Emotional intensity declines as baseline expectations reset

---

## Triggers

- Major purchases, promotions, achievements, or setbacks
- Lifestyle upgrades that quickly become routine
- Repeated exposure to previously exciting conditions
- Comparisons that continually update what feels normal

---

## Effects

- Lasting satisfaction from external gains is often weaker than expected
- People chase repeated upgrades for diminishing returns
- Losses can feel permanently devastating in the short term but soften over time
- Well-being depends partly on how quickly baselines reset

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - New salary:**
A raise feels transformative at first, then becomes the new normal within months.

**Example 2 - New device:**
A purchase that felt exciting quickly becomes background infrastructure once novelty fades.

**Example 3 - Social milestone:**
Recognition initially boosts mood, but the emotional lift weakens as expectations rise.

---

## Counters

- Avoid assuming large external changes will produce permanent emotional payoff
- Use gratitude and variety to slow adaptation
- Invest more in systems and relationships than in novelty-driven upgrades
