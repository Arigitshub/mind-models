# Classical Conditioning

**Category:** behavioral
**Origin:** Ivan Pavlov, 1897
**Tags:** conditioning, association, learning, stimulus, behavior

---

## Summary

Classical conditioning is learning by association, where a previously neutral cue comes to trigger a response after repeated pairing with a meaningful stimulus.

---

## Mechanism

1. An unconditioned stimulus naturally produces a response
2. A neutral stimulus is repeatedly paired with that unconditioned stimulus
3. The neutral stimulus becomes a conditioned stimulus
4. The conditioned stimulus begins to trigger a conditioned response on its own
5. Repetition strengthens the association until the cue reliably evokes the response

---

## Triggers

- Repeated pairing of cues and emotionally relevant events
- Environments with predictable stimulus patterns
- High-salience rewards, threats, or bodily responses
- Contexts where cues appear just before meaningful outcomes

---

## Effects

- Automatic responses to previously neutral cues
- Learned likes, dislikes, and anticipatory reactions
- Environmental triggers for stress, craving, or comfort
- Habit support through cue-linked expectation

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Notification sound:**
A person feels a small anticipatory jolt when hearing a phone chime because it has been repeatedly paired with rewarding messages.

**Example 2 - Exam anxiety:**
A classroom setting begins to trigger stress because it has been repeatedly paired with evaluation and pressure.

**Example 3 - Brand association:**
Advertising pairs a product with attractive imagery and music until the product itself starts carrying the positive feeling.

---

## Counters

- Break cue-outcome pairings when trying to weaken conditioned responses
- Re-pair old triggers with neutral or safer experiences
- Identify whether a reaction is driven by current reality or learned association
