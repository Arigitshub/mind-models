# Temptation Bundling

**Category:** behavioral
**Origin:** Katherine Milkman, Julia Minson, & Kevin Volpp, 2014
**Tags:** motivation, habit, reward, self-control, commitment

---

## Summary

Temptation bundling pairs an immediately rewarding activity with a beneficial but effortful behavior so the reward helps pull the desired behavior into action.

---

## Mechanism

1. Identify a behavior that is good long term but hard to start
2. Identify a separate activity that is immediately pleasurable
3. Restrict access to the pleasurable activity so it occurs only during the desired behavior
4. The immediate reward reduces resistance to starting
5. Over time, the desired behavior becomes easier to repeat

---

## Triggers

- Goals that fail because benefits are delayed
- Repetitive tasks with high friction and low immediate payoff
- Situations where self-control is depleted
- Opportunities to control when a reward is available

---

## Effects

- Increases adherence to difficult routines
- Makes delayed-benefit behaviors feel more attractive
- Reduces the activation energy for good habits
- Helps convert motivation into repeatable structure

---

## Examples

**Example 1 - Gym-only podcast:**
A person allows themselves to listen to a favorite thriller podcast only while exercising, making workouts easier to begin.

**Example 2 - Cleaning with entertainment:**
Someone watches a favorite show only while folding laundry, which turns a chore into a predictable combined ritual.

**Example 3 - Admin sprint:**
A founder saves a premium coffee for bookkeeping sessions, using the immediate reward to reduce avoidance.

---

## Counters

- Avoid bundling with rewards that overwhelm the main task and become the sole focus
- Use rewards that are appealing but sustainable rather than extreme treats
- Remove the bundle when the behavior is already automatic and no longer needs extra pull

---

## Related Models

- [Habit Loop](habit-loop.md) - bundled rewards strengthen the cue-routine-reward cycle
- [Operant Conditioning](operant-conditioning.md) - temptation bundling works by adding reinforcement to a target behavior
- [Loss Aversion](../decision/loss-aversion.md) - people often follow bundles more reliably when skipping feels like losing access to a reward
